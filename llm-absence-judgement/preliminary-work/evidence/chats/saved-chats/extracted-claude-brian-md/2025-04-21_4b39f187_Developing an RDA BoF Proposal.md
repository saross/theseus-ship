# RDA BoF Proposal: Standardizing LLM Interactions as Research Data

## Session Title
Documenting the Dialogue: LLM Interactions as Research Data

## Meeting Objectives
This Birds of a Feather session aims to establish frameworks for documenting, sharing, and standardizing Large Language Model (LLM) interactions within the research data lifecycle. As LLMs become integral to research processes across disciplines, the prompts and interactions themselves constitute valuable data artifacts that warrant standardization and preservation. Our objectives are to:

1. Frame LLM prompting as a formal research methodology requiring documentation standards comparable to code, protocols, and other methodology components, enabling transparency and reproducibility

2. Address the current lack of norms, formats, and tools for documenting AI use in research by establishing foundational principles for an "LLM interaction standard" that aligns with FAIR data principles

3. Develop preliminary specifications for formats and tools (such as bookmarklets or browser extensions) that could capture LLM interactions as structured, shareable research objects

4. Explore opportunities to extend existing frameworks (like RO-Crate) to incorporate LLM interaction documentation, and identify potential repository solutions for a community-maintained "grimoire" of research prompts

5. Initiate cross-disciplinary discussions that will form the basis for potential working groups focused on technical standards, ethical norms, and disciplinary best practices for LLM use throughout the research data lifecycle

This BoF distinguishes itself from existing RDA groups by specifically addressing the emerging need to treat researcher-LLM interactions as data artifacts worthy of standardization, documentation, and sharing within the open science ecosystem.
