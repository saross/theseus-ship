 The terms "techniques" and "technics" have different meanings and connotations, though they're related:

**Techniques** refers to specific methods, skills, or procedures used to accomplish tasks. This is the more common term in everyday language. Techniques are practical applications of knowledge - concrete ways of doing things.

**Technics** is a less common term with broader philosophical implications. It comes from the Greek "techne" and refers to the study of technology and technical processes as a whole. It often carries more theoretical weight, examining technology as a system or phenomenon rather than just specific methods.

Regarding the phrases:

"**Techniques and ethics of AI**" suggests a focus on:
- Specific AI methods and how they work
- The ethical implications of these specific methods
- A more practical, applied approach to both aspects

"**Technics and ethics of AI**" suggests:
- A broader examination of AI as a technological system
- How AI as a phenomenon relates to ethics
- A more philosophical or theoretical approach
- Potentially examining how AI technology transforms society and human experience

The second phrasing invites a deeper consideration of how AI as a technological system shapes our world, while the first focuses more on the practical applications and specific methods of AI alongside ethical considerations.
